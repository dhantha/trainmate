Replace Passenger Emergency Intercom
 
Applicable Cars
C Car, S Car, T Car
Equipment Condition
Wheels chocked.
Circuit breaker DOOR CONTROL CB33 set to OFF.
Circuit breaker PA AMPLIFIER CB47 set to OFF.
Circuit breaker COMMUNICATION SYSTEM CB48 set to OFF.
Circuit breaker SIGNS CB46 switched to OFF.

None.

Remove Passenger Emergency Intercom Panel


PEI Panel Replacement (Toilet Sidewall Shown)


  1. Remove four self-locking screws (2) and four countersunk washers (3) securing Passenger Emergency Intercom (PEI) panel (1) to the wall structure. Discard self-locking screws.
  1. Tilt top of unit (1) out of wall liner until back face of PEI body clears wall liner surface. If necessary, use a putty knife to help separate PEI flange from gasket (4).
  1. Lift unit (1) vertically to expose car wiring connections at bottom of PEI body and support in position.
  1. Disconnect three-part car wiring plug connected to connector (5). Tag and label disconnected car wiring plugs.
  1. Remove one nut, one lockwasher, and one washer securing carbody ground wire terminal to ground stud (6). Discard grounding lockwasher.
  1. Remove PEI panel (1) from cutout in wall liner.
Install PEI Panel
  1. Inspect condition of foam gasket (4) attached to wall liner. If damaged, discard and replace as follows.
  1. Scrape off old foam gasket from wall liner.
  1. Remove old foam and adhesive remnants by wiping with cloth soaked in mineral spirits.
  1. Wipe off any solvent residue with a clean, dry cloth and allow to air dry.
  1. Install new self-adhesive foam gasket (4).
  1. Position PEI panel (1) near cutout in wall liner and set carbody ground wire terminal on ground stud (5).
  1. Secure ground wire terminal to ground stud (5) with one nut, one new lockwasher, and one washer.
  1. Connect three-part car wiring plug to connector (5), positioned as indicated by labeled tags.
  1. Insert unit (1) into cutout, position leveled, and hold pressed against gasket (4).
  1. Secure PEI panel (1) to wall structure with four self-locking screws (2) and four countersunk washers (3)
Perform PEI Panel Power-up Test
  1. Restore power:
Switch circuit breaker DOOR CONTROL CB33 to ON.
Switch circuit breaker PA AMPLIFIER CB47 to ON.
Switch circuit breaker COMMUNICATION SYSTEM CB48 to ON.
  1. Activate cab by setting train operator console controls as follows.
  1. Master Controller keyed
  1. Master Controller Reverser set to F (Forward) to activate cab
  1. Master Controller Throttle switch set to 0 (Idle).
  1. In active cab, verify status of PEI panel (1) on Driver DIsplay Unit (DDU) as follows.


DDU Train Overview Screen


  1. From DDU Train Overview screen, click Local Process Data softkey button.


Typical DDU Process Data Screen


  1. From DDU Process Data screen for vehicle, ensure field PEI displays OK.
End of Replace
